# Methods note prospectus

## Working title

**From Backtest to Evidence: A Falsification-First Protocol for Machine Learning in Historical Markets**

## Core contribution

The empirical paper asks whether abnormal bookmaker actions predict future repricing. The methods note extracts the reusable research design:

1. separate mechanism evidence from realized-profit evidence;
2. fix candidate identity before adding flexible residual information;
3. use alignment-breaking placebos that preserve baseline structure and capacity;
4. test the full score distribution before optimizing a threshold;
5. model execution as a separate conversion layer;
6. audit deployment support before changing a live shadow stream;
7. activate repairs as parallel challengers rather than rewriting original evidence;
8. freeze a future cohort with immutable policy ledgers before targets mature.

## Why this can stand as a second paper

The note does not recycle the empirical result as a new contribution. Its unit of contribution is the **evidence architecture**. The football project is the worked example, while the proposed protocol is applicable to any historical market-ML system with repeated prices, agents and delayed targets.

## Proposed sections

### 1. The backtest identification problem

Historical market research often mixes four questions:

- is a statistical pattern present?
- is it attached to the correct entity and time?
- does it improve an economic decision at equal capacity?
- can the decision be executed prospectively?

A single return number cannot answer all four.

### 2. Fixed-identity residual learning

Define a raw model that selects the object and a residual model that may only rerank confidence for that object. This blocks flexible models from obtaining apparent uplift through identity substitution.

### 3. Structure-preserving falsification

Randomly permuting labels is often too easy. The stronger placebo preserves:

- baseline scores;
- chronological groups;
- score distribution;
- candidate identity;
- decision capacity;

while breaking only the hypothesized match-specific alignment.

### 4. Threshold-free evidence before strategy selection

Estimate a dose response within baseline-score strata. A top-tail policy is downstream only after the continuous relationship is established.

### 5. Three-layer endpoint hierarchy

- **Mechanism:** future same-agent price quality.
- **Economic conversion:** realized payoff at matched capacity.
- **Execution:** latency, fill, slippage, rejection and capacity.

Failure in a later layer does not erase evidence in an earlier layer, and success in an earlier layer cannot be marketed as success in a later one.

### 6. Deployment support and parallel repair

Measure feature support, panel breadth and timing compatibility before interpreting prospective performance. When a mismatch appears, preserve the original stream and activate a separately frozen challenger.

### 7. Immutable prospective closure

Write policy ledgers and hashes before future targets are joined. Predetermine volume, uncertainty, subgroup and concentration gates. Treat insufficient volume as an outcome, not permission to lower thresholds.

### 8. Worked example

Use the football market project to show:

- a mechanism that passes placebo and dose-response tests;
- a profit result that remains uncertain;
- an execution margin that fails under adverse fill;
- a deployment mismatch handled through parallel adapters;
- a future-only campaign that remains untouched.

## Candidate venues and formats

The first realistic form is a reproducibility or applied-methods preprint rather than a claim of a new universal statistical theorem. Suitable audiences include applied machine learning, computational social science, sports analytics and reproducible finance communities.

A later version should include one independent non-betting case study. The best extension would have multiple price-setting agents and a clearly observable later same-agent price.

## Completion criteria

The methods note is ready for external circulation when:

- the empirical paper's historical claim registry is stable;
- the seven-day campaign has a recorded result, including failure or insufficient volume;
- one independent reviewer has attempted the audit path;
- the MARB submission contract has at least one external or synthetic second profile;
- all diagrams and examples distinguish historical, prospective and operational evidence levels.
